Our History

Tenebo Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to eradicating Tuberculosis in Kenya and Africa. The organization was founded in January 2024 by Naserian Kasura, who was diagnosed with tuberculosis in August 2022. Drawing from her journey, she started this organization dedicated to tackling TB at its core. Her leadership is fueled by a unique blend of personal experience, compassion, and unwavering dedication. She is a living testament to the power of transforming personal hardship into a force for positive change in the fight against tuberculosis.

Our dedicated team is the engine that drives this Foundation forward. The team consists of a passionate group of individuals with diverse expertise in science, advocacy, education, and fundraising. Each member plays a vital role, from researchers seeking groundbreaking advancements to support staff ensuring resources reach those in need. United by a common goal of eradicating TB, our team's commitment and collaboration ensure the Foundation's impact continues to grow.

Tenebo was born from a simple truth: no one deserves to suffer from tuberculosis. This preventable and treatable disease still claims millions of lives each year, and we are dedicated to changing that. TB isn't just a medical issue; it has devastating social, psychological, and economic consequences. It traps families in poverty, disrupts education, and weakens individuals and communities. We believe in a holistic approach that addresses not only the disease itself but also the social determinants that perpetuate it.



Our Mission

Our mission is to empower communities most affected by tuberculosis through education, awareness and access to prevention, diagnosis and treatment services ultimately paving the way for a TB-free country and continent.



TENEBO GOALS & OBJECTIVES


Goal 1 : Reduce the incidence of TB in Kenya.

Objectives

  • Increase the rate of TB screening and case detection by 7% within the next year.
  • Decrease the time to diagnosis for individuals with suspected TB by 5%.
  • Ensure completion of TB treatment by 65% of diagnosed individuals through a mobile app system that reminds TB patients as well as education on the importance of completing treatment.

Goal 2 : Address the social determinants of health that contribute to TB risk

Objectives

  • Partner with relevant organizations to improve access to adequate housing, nutritious food, and clean water for vulnearble populations.
  • Implement educational programs addressiong the social and economic factors impacting TB transmission, such as poverty, malnutrition and lack of healthcare access.
  • Advocate for policies addressing social determinants of health at the local and national levels.
  • Advocate for policies addressing social determinants of health at the local and national levels.

Goal 3 : Control stigma and discrimination associated with TB

Objectives

  • Develop and launch awareness campaigns taht promote understanding and compassiontowards individuals with TB.
  • Partner with community leaders and individuals affected by TB to cahllenge stigma and discrimination.
  • Advocate for anti-discrimination policies and legal protection for individuals with TB.

Goal 4 : Strengthen the healthcare system's capacity to address TB.

Objectives

  • Train healthcare providers on best practices for TB diagnosis, treatmentand case management.
  • Advocate for increased funding for TB control programs and healthcare infrastructure improvements
  • Partner with healthcare facilities to improve access to diagnostic tools and medications essential for TB management.

Goal 5 : Foster collaboration and partnerships for a comprehensive response to TB.

Objectives

  • Establish and maintain partnerships with government agencies, healthcare providers ,community organizations and individuals affected by TB.
  • Collaborate on initiatives addressing various aspects of TB contol, such as prevention, diagnosis , treatment and social support,
  • Advocate for collective action and resource mobilization at the local ,national, and continental levels to achieve a TB-free future.

Goal 6 : Advance research and development efforts for TB control.

Objectives

  • Advocate for increased funding for research projects focused on developing new diagnostics, vaccines, and treatment options for TB.
  • Collaborative with researchers to ensure practical application of research findings in real-world esttings.
  • Raise public awareness about the importance of ongoing research and development in the fight against TB.

Goal 7 : Promote mental health support for individuals affected by TB.

Objectives

  • Develop and deliver mental health awareness and support programs for individuals diagnosed with TB and their families.
  • Advocate for access to affordable and culturally appropriate mental health services for TB patients.
  • Partner with mental health professionals to address the specific needs and challenges faced by individuals with TB.

Goal 8 : Promote digital health solutions for improved TB control

Objectives

  • Develop and implement mobile phone applications or online tools to facilitate TB screening, self-monitoring and medication adherence.
  • Train healthcare providers on utilizing digital platforms for effective TB management.
  • Ensure equitable access to technology and provide digital literacy training for vulnerable communities affected by TB.
